About Me

Hi, I'm Per, a Game Programmer with a strong interest in gameplay systems, technical problem-solving, and building interactive experiences from the ground up. I recently completed my studies in Games Programming and am currently looking to start my professional career in the games industry.

Throughout my studies, I worked with C++ and C#, primarily using Unreal Engine and Unity. My projects range from small 1st semester prototypes to more advanced 3rd semester projects, including a resource management game in Unreal Engine and multiple C++ console-based applications.

I enjoy working on gameplay mechanics such as player movement, combat systems, resource management, progression systems, and procedural elements. I am especially interested in understanding how systems interact with each other and how small design decisions affect the overall player experience.

From a personal standpoint, I consider myself a reliable team player who communicates clearly and values constructive feedback. I enjoy collaborating with designers and fellow programmers and feel comfortable explaining technical concepts in a clear and structured way.

I am highly self-motivated and curious, and I like to expand my skill set by building focused side projects or iterating on existing systems. Learning new tools, engines, or programming concepts is something I actively seek out, both during structured projects and in my own time.

My portfolio projects were created across multiple semesters: from a Unity-based 2D platformer in C# (1st semester), to a C# AVL tree implementation (2nd semester), and C++ console projects and a settler-style resource management game in Unreal Engine (3rd semester). Each project was designed to explore specific technical or gameplay-focused topics.

I am open to learning new technologies, adapting to existing codebases, and relocating or working internationally. My goal is to grow as a developer while contributing meaningful, well-structured code to a professional game development team.
